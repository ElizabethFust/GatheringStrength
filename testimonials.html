Get Inspired

Tom

BEFORE: Tom Standing

Tom has a C4 incomplete spinal cord injury. When Tom first began exercising, he had a hard time standing on his own.

AFTER: Tom Standing

After exercising for six months in the Community Fitness and Wellness gym at Frazier Rehab Institute, Tom is able to stand, even with resistance: note the bands over each shoulder, which are attached to weights providing resistance when he stands.

Raymond

Functional Electrical Stimulation and cardiovascular exercise

Raymond had a stroke in 2016. He uses functional electrical stimulation on his affected arm and leg to do upper-extremity and lower-extremity cycling. The stimulation activates the muscles to contract in the proper sequence for cycling. Also, with adaptive tools, such as a glove and a leg stabilizer, he gets cardiovascular exercise on a nustep recumbent stepper.

Liz

Weight training and Cardiovascular training

Liz is a T7 paraplegic. She suffered a spinal cord stroke in 2006. Once she was discharged from outpatient therapy, she realized that she needed to commmit to engaging in therapeutic exercise in order to maintain what she gained in outpatient therapy, and to ward off risk factors that lead to cardiovascular disease and diabetes.
